# Quality Rubric — PM Deliverables

Seven dimensions, each scored 1–10 with **cited evidence from the draft**. Overall = weighted average. Default weights shown; adjust at Round 0 by deliverable type with human approval.

## 1. Truthfulness & Grounding (weight 20%)
Every load-bearing claim is verified, sourced, or explicitly labeled as an assumption/inference. No fabricated metrics, no unverifiable superlatives ("fastest-growing"), no competitor capabilities asserted from memory or secondary sources.
- **10:** All claims traceable; verified-vs-inferred labeling explicit where it matters; competitive claims checked against primary sources.
- **7:** Claims are plausible and mostly sourced, but 1–2 assertions would not survive "where did that number come from?"
- **4:** Contains at least one falsifiable, load-bearing claim presented as fact. (Automatic P0.)

## 2. Focus & Narrative Spine (weight 15%)
One segment, one pain. A tight story with explicit sequencing ("why this first"). Nothing that dilutes the spine survives just because it's true.
- **10:** A reader can state the thesis in one sentence; every section serves it; cuts were made and are defensible.
- **7:** Clear thesis but 1–2 detours that a panel would experience as padding.
- **4:** Broad coverage substituting for a point of view.

## 3. Framework Rigor & Internal Consistency (weight 15%)
Named frameworks used correctly, not recited. Rule of 3, rule of 2: alternatives differ by *mechanism*, not packaging; the pick uses explicit criteria. Segmentation dimension stays constant throughout.
- **10:** Alternatives are genuinely different bets; prioritization criteria are stated and weighted; no dimension-switching.
- **7:** Framework present but one alternative is a strawman, or criteria are implied rather than stated.
- **4:** Framework name-dropping; alternatives are one idea in three coats. (Dimension-switch mid-argument = P0.)

## 4. User Empathy Before Business Outcome (weight 15%)
The pain is a genuine user experience, not a business problem in a user costume. Business wins emerge from solving the real problem, stated after it.
- **10:** Pain is specific, observable, and quotable from a real user's seat; the business case follows organically.
- **7:** Pain is real but generic; business framing occasionally leads.
- **4:** "Users are frustrated that our NRR is low."

## 5. Plain, Spoken Language (weight 10%)
Sounds like a sharp human talking. No consultant-speak, no jargon the audience wouldn't use, no AI cadences (em-dash chains, "delve," symmetrical triads, throat-clearing).
- **10:** Reads aloud naturally; contractions; concrete nouns; short sentences where it counts.
- **7:** Mostly clean with patches of formula.
- **4:** Would be flagged as AI-written or consultant boilerplate.

## 6. Defensibility Under Challenge (weight 15%)
Would survive the sharpest question in the room. Concedes what competitors already do, names what would change the author's mind, carries honest risks and trade-offs rather than a victory lap.
- **10:** The hardest objection is anticipated *in the deliverable*; concessions made proactively; "what would change my mind" is specific and falsifiable.
- **7:** Defensible but reactive — objections answerable, not preempted.
- **4:** A single obvious question collapses a load-bearing section.

## 7. Fit to the Core Ask (weight 10%)
Answers the actual brief/panel criteria — all of them, at the requested depth and length, without over-delivering beyond scope.
- **10:** Every stated ask is covered and findable; nothing asked-for is missing; nothing unasked-for bloats it.
- **7:** All asks covered but one is thin or buried.
- **4:** A stated ask is missing or the deliverable answers a different question. (P0.)

## Scoring rules
1. Quote the draft for every score. No quote, no score.
2. Overall ≥ 9.5 requires no dimension below 9.
3. Regardless of scores, list your **top 3 remaining weaknesses** with severity (P0/P1/P2). If you cannot name three, name as many real ones as exist — do not invent filler weaknesses, and do not omit real ones to protect the score.
